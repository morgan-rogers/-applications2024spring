• Trawler Coat
  • What UI elements did I see and/or interact with while finding, comparing, selecting, and configuring the specific product?
  • There were boxes of color that I clicked to view the product in different colorways
  • Arrows used to move between images
  • A tab below the image to switch back and forth between the male and female version of the coat
  • Sizes drop down after clicking sizes
  • A size guide pulls out on the right of the screen after clicking size guide
  • Have I seen this UI element before in other contexts?
  • I have seen these drop down boxes used in most product pages
  • Did I know what this element would do before I interacted with it? Why?
  • Due to convention, I knew what each element would do
  • What general problem is this element designed to handle?
  • The drop down boxes are used to avoid taking up too much space on the page/condense the information.
  • The image arrows are used so that the user can focus on one image at a time
  • Why was it used in this context? Is it successful?
  • The arrows for viewing multiple images is useful, although I think it would be nice to see little thumbnails of all of the images as well
  • The content makes up only about 20% of the page, whereas the images of the coat take up the rest. This is different from what is usually done on clothing websites, and although it looks interesting, if the content was increased to about 30% with larger text it would be more effective
  • Usually size guides are at the bottom of the page. I like how this one pulls out to the right of the image, it makes it harder to miss out on.

  • Florasis Lipstick
  • What UI elements did I see and/or interact with while finding, comparing, selecting, and configuring the specific product?
  • Boxes indicating the different shades of lipstick
  • A drop down where the user can select the lipstick shade they want
  • A counter to add multiple lipsticks to your cart
  • A heart icon to indicate adding the item to a favorites list
  • A panel of thumbnail images to the left
  • There are arrows above and below the images but they aren’t clickable
  • Did I know what this element would do before I interacted with it? Why?
  • Due to convention, I knew what each element would do
  • Due to the direction of the thumbnail images though and the fact that there is no indicator of which image you are currently on, I was not sure which image the arrows would move me to
  • What general problem is this element designed to handle?
  • The thumbnail show the user the variety of images they can view at a glance
  • Why was it used in this context? Is it successful?
  • For each different shade of lipstick, there is not a large variation of images that can be seen. There are no swatches or different skin tones, so most users would have a hard time differentiating the lipsticks and figuring out what it would look like on them
  • The reviews can be easily viewed below the product which is very helpful

  • Traeger Grill
  • What UI elements did I see and/or interact with while finding, comparing, selecting, and configuring the specific product?
  • There are two large boxes indicating that there are two sizes with different prices
  • “Add Essentials” buttons comes before “Add to Cart”, prompting users to make additional purchases along with the main item.
  • Two circles indicate the different colorways
  • Bold text with catchy phrases indicate the key features of the product
  • A visual with measurements shows the size
  • Why was it used in this context? Is it successful?
  • The two large boxes that indicate the size are useful because it emphasizes important information. But, since the size is a big distinction, it is confusing that when you click between the two sizes the images that appear do not give instant visual context to the size difference
  • The large gallery of images and videos are effective
  • Did I know what this element would do before I interacted with it? Why?
  • Due to convention, I knew what each element would do
  • What general problem is this element designed to handle?
  • There is a lot of complex information that goes along with picking out a grill to buy. The large visuals, simple and bold text, visual illustrations, and easy to add on essential purchases makes the product easier to digest

  • Ikea Lamp
  • What UI elements did I see and/or interact with while finding, comparing, selecting, and configuring the specific product?
  • Images are arranged in a grid of four, all equal sizes
  • When you hover over the color, it change the first image lamp
  • “Complete with” tab suggests to the user that they buy a lightbulb, which is completed by clicking a large plus sign
  • Suggested items appear below
  • A heart icon indicates a favorites list
  • The “Add to Cart” button is next to a counter
  • Why was it used in this context? Is it successful?
  • Since there are only a few images, the grid (rather than having one image with thumbnails) is successful because I learn more about the item at a quick glance
  • I think the height of the lamp could be emphasized more
  • Did I know what this element would do before I interacted with it? Why?
  • There are two ways to choose color: clicking the images, or pressing a drop down menu that says “Choose Color”. In this case it feels a bit repetitive and I was unsure which to click.
  • Every other element acted as expected
  • What general problem is this element designed to handle?
  • A lamp is a simple item with not a ton of information needed to intake. The design is simple and effective, especially due to the way the images are arranged

  • What elements or patterns seem universal?
  • Images and content create two distinct sections of the page, with the images most typically being on the right and taking up a larger portion of the screen.
  • Drop down tabs are very common amongst all pages to add detail and show different types of information, whether it be shipping, product details, or specifications.
  • Did I see anything that felt different or unusual?
  • The coat website comes from a more niche brand, and it is clear that they wanted to do something different from typical design patterns. The content is much more condensed in a tight column, and the page is image heavy.
  • What elements or patterns were specific to the store or product?
  • Specific to IKEA was the “How to get it” section which is shown on all of their items
  • Products that typically must be bought with additional items had some sort of design that suggested additional products and make it easy to add to cart
  • Were there any patterns or techniques that made one store stand out? Could the other stores have applied that technique successfully?
  • Once again, the coat website stood out because its design was a bit more unique. I think this worked for a clothing brand, where they want to emphasize aesthetic, lifestyle, and style. But for a grill or lamp, this would not apply as well. I think the lipstick company could possibly use the technique.
  • Finally, how did you feel when interacting with these sites? When were you happiest/most comfortable? When were you disrupted/confused/bothered?
  • I generally feel like IKEA’s website feels a bit hectic to me, just because of all of the text and tabs. I felt the same for the grill website. The text and images were so large and I wanted to see less. I felt most comfortable at the clothing and makeup websites.
